Strawberry horchata

Horchata is a classic! A delicious, creamy, rice beverage kissed by a touch of vanilla extract. Imagine the flavors of horchata paired with strawberries. And, in return you get a drink that resembles the flavors of ice cream. In fact, this agua fresca is not only delicious but also easy to make. Love horchata? This recipe is a win-win situation.

Course: Drinks // Cuisine: Mexican // Difficulty: Easy

Servings: 12 servings
Prep time: 10 minutes
Soaking time: 4 hours
Total time: 4 hours 10 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of long-grain rice
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 cups of water, boiling/hot
  • 1 12 oz can of evaporated milk
  • 1 14 oz can of sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup of whole milk
  • 1.5 lb fresh strawberries, cleaned
  • 4 cups of water, room temperature
  • 2 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • Sugar, to taste
  • Ice
  • Strawberries, diced for garnish

Instructions

  1. Pour the rice in a strainer and rinse remove excess starch. Drain all of the water and pour into a heat proof deep container. Break the cinnamon stick and add it in. Pour in the 2 cups of boiling water, it should cover the rice completely. Cover with plastic wrap and allow it to soak for 4 hrs or all night.
  2. Blend the rice, water and cinnamon stick until completely broken down. Strain into a pitcher.
  3. Slice off and discard the green leaves from the strawberries. Roughly cut them and blend with the 4 cups of room temperature water until completely smooth. Pour into the pitcher.
  4. Pour in the evaporated, condensed and whole milk. Add the vanilla extract. Mix to combine.
  5. Serve over ice and top it with diced strawberries.
  6. ENJOY!
